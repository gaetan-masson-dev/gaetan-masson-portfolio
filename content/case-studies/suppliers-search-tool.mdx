## Overview

Our B2B platform connected buyers with suppliers across multiple industries (manufacturing, food service, hospitality). The original search tool was built for a single vertical and struggled as we expanded to serve diverse industries with different search needs.

## The Challenge

The existing search experience was causing significant friction:

- Users from different industries had completely different mental models
- Search results weren't relevant because filters didn't match industry expectations
- No way to search across multiple verticals simultaneously
- High bounce rate on search results page (65%)
- Customer support was overwhelmed with "can't find what I need" tickets

The core issue: we had bolted on new industries to a search tool designed for one specific vertical, creating a confusing and ineffective experience.

## Research

We conducted extensive research to understand how different user types approached supplier search:

### User Interviews

Interviewed 30 buyers across manufacturing, food service, and hospitality:

- Manufacturing buyers searched by technical specifications (materials, certifications, capacity)
- Food service buyers prioritized location, delivery capabilities, and product categories
- Hospitality buyers focused on style, customization, and designer collections

### Competitive Analysis

Analyzed search patterns on industry-specific marketplaces to understand established conventions.

### Search Analytics

Examined 6 months of search data:

- 40% of searches returned zero results
- Users abandoned search after 2.3 refinements on average
- Most common queries mixed industry-specific terminology our system didn't understand

## Solution

We redesigned the search experience around user mental models rather than our internal vertical categorization.

### Unified Search Entry

Created a smart search that automatically detected industry context:

- Natural language processing to understand industry-specific terminology
- Auto-suggestion adapted to user's industry and search history
- Option to search across all verticals or filter to specific industries

### Adaptive Filters

Filters dynamically changed based on search context:

- Manufacturing searches showed technical filters (certifications, materials, MOQ)
- Food service searches prioritized location, delivery, and categories
- Hospitality searches highlighted style, customization, and designer options

This context-aware approach meant users always saw relevant filters without being overwhelmed by irrelevant options.

### Improved Results

Redesigned result cards to surface information most relevant to each vertical:

- Manufacturing: Certifications and technical capabilities upfront
- Food service: Location, delivery radius, product range
- Hospitality: Visual showcase of work, style tags, customization options

### Search Refinement

Added visual feedback for applied filters and suggested refinements based on common patterns from successful searches.

## Testing & Validation

Conducted usability testing with 15 participants across all verticals:

- Task completion rate improved from 45% to 87%
- Time to find relevant supplier reduced from 8 minutes to 2.5 minutes
- Users described the new search as "intuitive" and "actually understands what I need"

## Results

The redesigned search tool significantly improved user success:

- Bounce rate reduced from 65% to 28%
- Zero-result searches decreased from 40% to 8%
- Support tickets about search dropped by 70%
- User engagement with supplier profiles increased by 120%

## Key Learnings

- **Context is everything**: Generic filters don't work when user needs vary dramatically by industry
- **Match mental models**: Using industry-specific terminology improved comprehension immediately
- **Progressive disclosure**: Showing relevant options based on context reduced cognitive load
- **Test with real scenarios**: Usability testing with industry-specific tasks revealed issues we hadn't anticipated
